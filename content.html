Environment

Minting a single NFT can cost the same amount of energy as a two-hour flight.

The environmental impact is enormous in the crypto space. The energy consumption of the computers used to mine Bitcoins alone is estimated at 50% of the world's energy consumption. However, we don't have to worry about that, since literally all NFT art runs on the Ether Chain. Unfortunately, Ether is still running on proof-of-work technology and is therefore not much better than Bitcoin. One can only hope that Ether manages to switch to proof of stake in the 3rd quarter of 2022. I myself have lost hope and prefer to wait for Tupac to make his comeback. But sometime in the next 5-10 years Ethereum should succeed.... for sure ;)

Web3

"If NFTs suck so much, then why are they so popular?"


To answer that, lets anwer why web3 doesn't suck: decentralisation. Pretty much any pros for web3 people come up with, originates from the above. Why do we need decentralisation? To remove the trust component of transactions. How? In simple words: by having multiple synced sources of information about ownership of assets (crypto money, data) who all abide by the "smart contract", no single party can start a "he said, she said" war, because they cover eachothers integrity. This leads to the mentioned pros: data ownership, transparency, security (in some ways) and many, many more, of which a good portion are actually double edged swords. Now lets see what it costs to have all those wonderful things: - redundancy by storing data on multiple host points, means youre chiming in for every transaction of yours stored on the blockchain. That comes in the form of Gas prices - anymosity means is for everyone, that means you could be dealing with corrupt parties without knowing it - by having more complexity and abstraction, people are much easier to trick into making mistakes - you need a computer and blockchain knowledge to deal in crypto currency - while a single entity can't regulate supply of crypto currency (given a propper smart contract), a random tweet from some guy you never heard of can still influence demand, leaving you with some data no one wants to give you reasonable amounts of currency or goods for anymore - while the whole thing might be decentralized, the smart contract is written by a single party. this smart contract can be annoying to find and is like fine print but much worse And to go even further - Web3 transactions are slow, because they have to sync between all hosts. Its sometimes hard to tell whether the transaction has stopped for some reason or is still ongoing - It is chaotic and almost impossible to control and regulate - Its a pain to develop (speaking from experience here) - Blockchains can't talk to each other, which means your wallet and assets are stuck on that particular blockchain - The whole thing has horrible UX - People are abusing these problems for scams and a quick buck relentlessly And then theres NFTs which add a whole other layer problems.
